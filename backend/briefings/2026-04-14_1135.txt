The five macroprudential capital buffers are: the Capital Conservation Buffer (CCoB), which is intended to improve banks’ general loss absorbing capacity; the Global Systemically Important Institution (G-SII) and Other Systemically Important Institution (O-SII) buffers, which address the risk that the failure of systemically important institutions may pose to the economy and the financial system as a whole; the Countercyclical Capital Buffer (CCyB), which is aimed at building resilience to cyclical systemic risks; and the Systemic Risk Buffer (SyRB), which targets systemic risks not covered by other instruments.
