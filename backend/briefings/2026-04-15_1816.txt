Last year’s One Big Beautiful Bill Act introduced those benefits and others for millions of Americans. The new tax law includes a higher cap on thestate and local tax deduction, a new deduction for up to $10,000 ofcar loan interest, as well as deductions fortipsandovertime. Seniors also get a new break: an additional$6,000 deductionfor people over 65, subject to income limits. You can claim it whether you take thestandard deduction or itemize. The federal government so far has received more than 134 million individual income tax returns this filing season, with the vast majority filed electronically, Internal Revenue Service CEO Frank Bisignano said on Wednesday in testimony before the Senate Committee on Finance. The average refund amount clocked in at over $3,400 — an 11% jump over last year — mainly due to the new deductions in the One Big Beautiful Bill Act. According to the Treasury Department: More than 6 million filers have claimed “no tax on tips,” with an average deduction of $7,100. More than 25 million filers have claimed “no tax on overtime,” with an average deduction of over $3,100. More than 30 million seniors have claimed the enhanced deduction. More than 1 million filers have claimed the deduction on car loan interest, with an average deduction of $11,800. “Working families across our nation are benefitting from pro-growth policies like never before," Bisignana told senators. Typically, about 20% of filers wait until the very last day to file, Lisa Greene-Lewis, CPA and TurboTax expert, told Yahoo Finance. That share was even higher this year. But higher refunds should provide an extra incentive for taxpayers to file and pay their bills by the 11:59 p.m. deadline. Emma Ockermanis a reporter covering the economy and labor for Yahoo Finance. Kinder Morgan currently has $10 billion of commercially secured expansions in its backlog. About 90% of its backlog is new natural gas pipelines and related infrastructure that should enter commercial service by the middle of 2030. These projects will grow the company's earnings in the coming years, giving it more fuel to increase its dividend, which it has done for nine years in a row. They will grow the company's earnings, supporting its plans to increase its dividend by 3% to 4% per year.
